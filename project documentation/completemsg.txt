ive‑in Home Support (Non‑Personal Care)
Who this is for
Our live‑in home support is for adults and older people in Barnet and Enfield who:

Want to stay in their own home but feel safer with someone there day and night.

Are lonely, anxious or struggling to manage day‑to‑day tasks alone.

Have a family carer who needs more rest or can’t always be there.

It’s ideal when visiting support isn’t quite enough, but full personal care or a care home still feels like too big a step.

What a live‑in support worker can do
Your live‑in support worker stays in the home, offering:

Companionship and reassurance – someone to talk to, spend time with and share everyday life.

A calm, steady presence – especially helpful if you feel anxious, low or unsettled.

Light household help – tidying, laundry, washing up, simple meal preparation.

Shopping and errands – food shopping, collecting prescriptions, posting parcels, small jobs.

Support around appointments – helping to arrange and, if agreed, escorting to GP or hospital visits.

Gentle prompts and reminders – for meals, drinks, taking prescribed medicines (you remain responsible for the medicines themselves).

The exact shape of the week is planned around you and your routines.

What’s not included (important)
This live‑in service is non‑regulated home support. It does not include:

Personal care such as washing, bathing or showering.

Help with dressing or undressing.

Toileting or continence care.

Hands‑on medication administration, injections or dressings.

Any nursing or clinical procedures.

If you need personal care or nursing support, we will always be honest about that and can signpost you towards appropriate services.

How it works in practice
A carefully chosen support worker moves into your home for an agreed period (short‑term or long‑term).

They have their own room and reasonable time for rest each day.

Together, we agree what a typical day looks like – when they’re usually busy helping, and when they’re more “on hand” if needed.

We stay in touch with you and your family to make sure things are working well.

All our support workers are:

Self‑employed, but selected and guided by Friendly Support Limited.

Interviewed, background‑checked and enhanced DBS‑checked.

Given training in dignity, communication and supporting adults and older people at home.

Price guide
Our live‑in home support is priced per week, depending on needs and location.

As a guide:

Live‑in home support (no personal care) starts from around £1,150 per week.

This covers:

A live‑in support worker in the home day and night.

Companionship, light household help, shopping/errands and support around appointments.

Our matching, checks and ongoing oversight.

We’ll always talk through your specific situation and give you a clear weekly price before anything starts.

What we can help with

Intro

We offer non‑regulated home support. That means we help with the practical and social side of life, but we don’t provide personal care or any nursing procedures. Our visits are unhurried (minimum 2 hours), and we can also arrange live‑in home support for people who feel safer with someone in the house.

Regular visits
Companionship and social visits
Friendly visits at home or a short trip out – a cuppa, a chat, a walk, a game or whatever makes the day feel brighter.

Sitting service for family carers
We stay with your loved one so you can have a break, run errands or simply rest, knowing someone reliable is there.

Support getting to appointments
Help with getting to and from GP, hospital or other appointments, including company in the waiting room if needed.

Shopping and errands
A hand with the weekly shop, collecting prescriptions, posting parcels and other everyday jobs that are getting harder to manage alone.

Light domestic support
Gentle help at home – tidying, laundry, washing up and simple meal preparation – to keep things comfortable and safe.

Live‑in home support (non‑personal care)
Live‑in home support – short‑term or long‑term
For some people, knowing there is someone in the house day and night makes all the difference. We can arrange short‑term or longer‑term live‑in home support, where a carefully chosen support worker stays in the home to offer company and practical help.

Live‑in support can include companionship, light housework, simple meal preparation, shopping, errands and support around appointments. It does not include personal care (such as washing, dressing or continence support) or any nursing procedures, and is therefore a non‑regulated service with no CQC involvement.

What we don’t do
We currently provide non‑regulated support only. We don’t offer:

Personal care such as washing, dressing or continence support

Giving medication or doing dressings

Any nursing or medical procedures

If you’re not sure which side your needs fall on, we’re happy to talk it through and point you in the right direction if we’re not the right fit.

About page
Heading

A bit about us

Who we are

Friendly Support Limited is a small, local service for people in Barnet and Enfield who want to stay in their own homes but would value a bit of extra help and company. We keep things simple, personal and flexible – no call centres, no rushed 15‑minute visits.

Your background (no doctor capacity)

Friendly Support Limited was started by someone who has spent many years working in the NHS with adults and older people living with long‑term health conditions. That experience means we understand how health problems can affect everyday life, and how important it is to feel listened to and treated with respect.

Through Friendly Support Limited we are not offering medical care or medical advice. We don’t diagnose, prescribe or provide treatments, and we’re not acting in the capacity of a doctor or specialist. Our role here is purely to offer practical and social support alongside your usual NHS and social care services.

Our team

We work with a small group of self‑employed home support workers (HCAs) who are:

Properly interviewed and background‑checked

Given training in communication, dignity and supporting people who may be frail, anxious or forgetful

Given clear guidance for each visit and regular check‑ins

Wherever possible, we match you with the same familiar faces so trust can build over time.

Our values

Kindness – turning up as we would for our own family.

Honesty – being upfront about what we can and can’t do.

Reliability – on time, as agreed, every visit.

Respect – working with you, not over you.

Regulation – clear and simple

At the moment, Friendly Support Limited offers non‑regulated home support, which doesn’t require registration with the Care Quality Commission (CQC). Our long‑term plan is to build on this foundation and, once fully ready and approved, offer regulated personal care as well – but for now, we stick firmly to the non‑regulated side.
